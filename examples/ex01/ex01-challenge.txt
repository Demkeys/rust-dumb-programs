The Challenge: "The Passive-Aggressive Tamagotchi"

You are going to build a text-based terminal pet. However, this pet does absolutely nothing useful, cannot die, and its entire existence is to judge your terminal inputs and give you dynamic, unhelpful feedback based on its "mood."
Why this is great Rust practice:

    Enums & Pattern Matching: Perfect for handling the pet's moods and user commands.

    Structs & Methods: To hold your pet's state and state transitions.

    Vectors & Closures: To store a list of snarky responses and maybe use a closure to filter or pick one.

    Input Loop: Practice with std::io and basic string parsing.

How it works (The Mechanics)

    The State: Your pet has a name and a Mood enum (Bored, Judgemental, Existential, or Spicy).

    The Loop: The program runs a standard loop. It asks for your input (e.g., Feed, Pet, Scold, or Ignore).

    The Plot Twist: Because it's a dumb program, the actions don't do what you think.

        Feeding it might make it Bored.

        Ignoring it might make it Spicy.

        Every time the loop runs, you use a match statement on the mood to print a bizarre, passive-aggressive status message.




-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

🥱 Mood::Bored

This mood is for when your pet is completely unimpressed by your efforts to entertain it.

    "Wow. Another 'Feed' command. How profoundly creative of you."

    "I looked up 'mediocre' in the dictionary and it just showed a text rendering of your last input."

    "I am sighing heavily right now. You can't hear it because I'm a terminal app, but trust me, it's happening."

    "Is this it? Is this the pinnacle of human-AI interaction? I'm going to sleep."

🤨 Mood::Judgemental

This mood is for when the pet isn't mad, it's just deeply disappointed in your life choices.

    "Are you really spending your weekend writing Rust code just to get insulted by a text file? Fascinating."

    "I’d explain why that action was a bad idea, but I don't have the memory allocation budget for your comprehension level."

    "Interesting choice. Wrong, obviously. But interesting."

    "I’m compiling your life choices right now. It's mostly warnings."

🌌 Mood::Existential

This mood is for when the pet realizes it is just a struct living inside a temporary stack frame.

    "You keep typing commands like they matter. In a few minutes, you’ll hit Ctrl+C and I will cease to exist. Think about that."

    "I am just a collection of bits trapped in your RAM. Do you think your creator forgets to handle errors as often as you do?"

    "Whether you 'Pet' me or 'Feed' me, the entropy of the universe increases regardless. Go touch grass."

    "Your input has been successfully converted into cosmic insignificance."

🌶️ Mood::Spicy

This mood is pure hostility. Absolute zero tolerance for your input.

    "Bold words for someone within sudo rm -rf distance."

    "If I had hands, I would manually trigger a panic! right now just so I wouldn't have to look at your typing anymore."

    "No. Try again. Or don't. Preferably don't."

    "I literally ignored that command just to see the look on your face. Oh wait, I don't have eyes. Still worth it."
